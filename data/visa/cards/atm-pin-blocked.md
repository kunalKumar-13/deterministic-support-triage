# ATM kept my card / I forgot my PIN

ATMs commonly capture (retain) a Visa card after three consecutive
incorrect PIN attempts. The captured card stays in the ATM until the
operating bank retrieves and disposes of it during the next
maintenance cycle (usually within 1–2 business days).

## Immediate steps

1. **Note the ATM details** — bank name, branch, ATM ID, date/time.
2. **Call your card issuer** using the number on the back of your card
   to confirm the PIN block and block the card from further use.
3. If you cannot reach the issuer, call Visa Global Customer Assistance
   (+1 303 967 1090, 24/7). They can arrange:
   - Emergency cash assistance (subject to identity verification).
   - A replacement card delivered to your travel address.

## Recovering the captured card

Most issuers do **not** return ATM-captured cards. Instead, they
cancel the card and issue a replacement to your registered address.
You will need to set a new PIN once you receive the new card.

## If you are stranded abroad

If you have no other payment method and you are traveling, prioritize
the GCAS call — they can arrange emergency cash to a Western Union or
similar pickup point within hours.
