# "The merchant says I have to spend a minimum amount on my Visa card"

In many markets, **Visa's network rules prohibit merchants from
imposing a minimum-purchase amount for credit-card transactions**.
However, US-based merchants may set a minimum of up to $10 for
**credit** card transactions under federal law — and that minimum
cannot apply to **debit** cards.

## US Virgin Islands and other US territories

In US territories (including the US Virgin Islands), merchants are
allowed to set a minimum of up to **$10 for credit-card transactions**.
They are **not** allowed to:

- Set a minimum higher than $10.
- Apply a minimum to debit-card transactions.
- Apply a minimum that is different for different card brands (e.g.
  Visa vs Mastercard).

## What you can do

1. Confirm the merchant is honoring the $10 cap and applying it only
   to credit cards.
2. If you believe the merchant is violating the rule, you can:
   - Pay using a different method and walk away.
   - Report the merchant to your card issuer with the merchant name
     and transaction details; the issuer may pursue the case with
     Visa's merchant-compliance team.

Outside US territories the rules vary by country; check with your
issuer if you encounter the practice abroad.
