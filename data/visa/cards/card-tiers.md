# Visa card tiers — Classic, Gold, Platinum, Signature, Infinite

Visa cards are issued in several tiers, each with a different set of
benefits. The tier is decided by the **issuing bank**, not Visa
directly. Visa sets the minimum benefits standard for each tier.

## Common tiers (consumer credit/debit)

- **Visa Classic** — entry-level, basic Zero Liability protection.
- **Visa Gold** — higher card limits, extended warranty perks.
- **Visa Platinum** — travel-related perks, lounge passes in some
  markets.
- **Visa Signature** — enhanced travel benefits, complimentary travel
  insurance in many markets.
- **Visa Infinite** — top tier; complimentary travel insurance, hotel
  upgrades, concierge.

The exact perks per tier vary by market and issuer. The complimentary
insurance benefits that some travel policies require typically only
apply at the **Signature** and **Infinite** tiers.

## How to check your tier

1. Look at the front of your card — the tier name (e.g. "Platinum",
   "Signature") is usually printed below the Visa logo.
2. Check your card's terms-and-conditions document from your issuing
   bank.
3. Call the customer-service number on the back of the card if you
   cannot find it.

## Upgrading

Tier upgrades are handled by your issuing bank, not Visa. Contact your
bank to request a tier change; they will assess eligibility based on
spending history, credit profile, and product availability.
