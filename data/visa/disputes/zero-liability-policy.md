# Visa Zero Liability Policy

Visa's Zero Liability Policy protects cardholders from unauthorized
transactions where the cardholder is **not at fault**. The policy is
applied by the card-**issuing bank**, not by Visa directly.

## What is covered

- Unauthorized signature transactions and online transactions where the
  cardholder did not authorize the charge.
- Transactions reported promptly to the issuing bank.

## What may NOT be covered

- Transactions where the cardholder shared their PIN, card details, or
  one-time passcode with another person.
- Cases where the cardholder is found grossly negligent (e.g.
  written PIN on the card).
- Commercial card products in some markets — coverage rules differ.
- Some PIN-based transactions, depending on the issuer's
  implementation. Issuers in different markets apply the policy
  differently; check with your bank.

## If your bank refuses to apply Zero Liability

You can:

1. Ask the bank in writing for the specific reason for refusal, citing
   Visa's Zero Liability Policy.
2. File a chargeback (separate from the Zero Liability claim) if the
   transaction meets chargeback criteria.
3. Escalate to your local banking regulator if you believe the bank
   misapplied the policy.

Visa does not adjudicate Zero Liability claims between cardholder and
issuer — the issuer makes the final call.
