# Visa cardholder fraud protection — what to do

If you see charges on your Visa card statement that you did not
authorize:

1. **Contact your card issuer immediately** using the number on the
   back of your card. They will block the card and start a fraud
   investigation.
2. **Identify the unauthorized transactions** — date, amount, merchant
   name as it appears on the statement.
3. **Ask the issuer to apply Visa's Zero Liability protection** where
   applicable.
4. **Open a dispute** for each fraudulent transaction (separate from
   the Zero Liability claim).
5. **File a police report** if your jurisdiction requires one or if the
   amounts are significant.

## Identity theft

If your identity has been stolen (someone has opened accounts or made
charges using your personal information):

1. Contact each of your card issuers and ask them to flag the
   account for identity theft.
2. File a police report and request a case number.
3. Contact your local credit bureaus and place a fraud alert or
   security freeze on your file.
4. Report the incident to the relevant national fraud authority (e.g.
   FTC IdentityTheft.gov in the US; local cybercrime portal in
   India).

Visa itself does not investigate consumer identity theft cases — that
is the role of the issuing bank, your local fraud bureau, and law
enforcement. Visa supports issuers with network-level fraud monitoring.
